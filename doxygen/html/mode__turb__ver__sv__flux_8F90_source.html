22!!**** *TURB_VER_SV_FLUX* -compute the source terms due to the vertical turbulent
23!! fluxes.
24!!
25!! PURPOSE
26!! -------
27! The purpose of this routine is to compute the vertical turbulent
28! fluxes of the evolutive variables and give back the source
29! terms to the main program. In the case of large horizontal meshes,
30! the divergence of these vertical turbulent fluxes represent the whole
31! effect of the turbulence but when the three-dimensionnal version of
32! the turbulence scheme is activated (CTURBDIM="3DIM"), these divergences
33! are completed in the next routine TURB_HOR.
34! An arbitrary degree of implicitness has been implemented for the
35! temporal treatment of these diffusion terms.
36! The vertical boundary conditions are as follows:
37! * at the bottom, the surface fluxes are prescribed at the same
38! as the other turbulent fluxes
39! * at the top, the turbulent fluxes are set to 0.
40! It should be noted that the condensation has been implicitely included
41! in this turbulence scheme by using conservative variables and computing
42! the subgrid variance of a statistical variable s indicating the presence
43! or not of condensation in a given mesh.
44!
45!!** METHOD
46!! ------
47!! 1D type calculations are made;
48!! The vertical turbulent fluxes are computed in an off-centered
49!! implicit scheme (a Crank-Nicholson type with coefficients different
50!! than 0.5), which allows to vary the degree of implicitness of the
51!! formulation.
52!! The different prognostic variables are treated one by one.
53!! The contributions of each turbulent fluxes are cumulated into the
54!! tendency PRvarS, and into the dynamic and thermal production of
55!! TKE if necessary.
56!!
57!! In section 2 and 3, the thermodynamical fields are considered.
58!! Only the turbulent fluxes of the conservative variables
59!! (Thetal and Rnp stored in PRx(:,:,:,1)) are computed.
60!! Note that the turbulent fluxes at the vertical
61!! boundaries are given either by the soil scheme for the surface one
62!! ( at the same instant as the others fluxes) and equal to 0 at the
63!! top of the model. The thermal production is computed by vertically
64!! averaging the turbulent flux and multiply this flux at the mass point by
65!! a function ETHETA or EMOIST, which preform the transformation from the
66!! conservative variables to the virtual potential temperature.
67!!
68!! In section 4, the variance of the statistical variable
69!! s indicating presence or not of condensation, is determined in function
70!! of the turbulent moments of the conservative variables and its
71!! squarred root is stored in PSIGS. This information will be completed in
72!! the horizontal turbulence if the turbulence dimensionality is not
73!! equal to "1DIM".
74!!
75!! In section 5, the x component of the stress tensor is computed.
76!! The surface flux <u'w'> is computed from the value of the surface
77!! fluxes computed in axes linked to the orography ( i", j" , k"):
78!! i" is parallel to the surface and in the direction of the maximum
79!! slope
80!! j" is also parallel to the surface and in the normal direction of
81!! the maximum slope
82!! k" is the normal to the surface
83!! In order to prevent numerical instability, the implicit scheme has
84!! been extended to the surface flux regarding to its dependence in
85!! function of U. The dependence in function of the other components
86!! introduced by the different rotations is only explicit.
87!! The turbulent fluxes are used to compute the dynamic production of
88!! TKE. For the last TKE level ( located at PDZZ(:,:,IKB)/2 from the
89!! ground), an harmonic extrapolation from the dynamic production at
90!! PDZZ(:,:,IKB) is used to avoid an evaluation of the gradient of U
91!! in the surface layer.
92!!
93!! In section 6, the same steps are repeated but for the y direction
94!! and in section 7, a diagnostic computation of the W variance is
95!! performed.
96!!
97!! In section 8, the turbulent fluxes for the scalar variables are
98!! computed by the same way as the conservative thermodynamical variables
99!!
100!!
101!! EXTERNAL
102!! --------
103!! GX_U_M, GY_V_M, GZ_W_M : cartesian gradient operators
104!! GX_U_UW,GY_V_VW (X,Y,Z) represent the direction of the gradient
105!! _(M,U,...)_ represent the localization of the
106!! field to be derivated
107!! _(M,UW,...) represent the localization of the
108!! field derivated
109!!
110!!
111!! MXM,MXF,MYM,MYF,MZM,MZF
112!! : Shuman functions (mean operators)
113!! DXF,DYF,DZF,DZM
114!! : Shuman functions (difference operators)
115!!
116!! SUBROUTINE TRIDIAG : to compute the split implicit evolution
117!! of a variable located at a mass point
118!!
119!! SUBROUTINE TRIDIAG_WIND: to compute the split implicit evolution
120!! of a variable located at a wind point
121!!
122!! FUNCTIONs ETHETA and EMOIST :
123!! allows to compute:
124!! - the coefficients for the turbulent correlation between
125!! any variable and the virtual potential temperature, of its
126!! correlations with the conservative potential temperature and
127!! the humidity conservative variable:
128!! ------- ------- -------
129!! A' Thv' = ETHETA A' Thl' + EMOIST A' Rnp'
130!!
131!!
132!! IMPLICIT ARGUMENTS
133!! ------------------
134!! Module MODD_CST : contains physical constants
135!!
136!! CST%XG : gravity constant
137!!
138!! Module MODD_CTURB: contains the set of constants for
139!! the turbulence scheme
140!!
141!! CSTURB%XCMFS,XCMFB : cts for the momentum flux
142!! CSTURB%XCSHF : ct for the sensible heat flux
143!! CSTURB%XCHF : ct for the moisture flux
144!! CSTURB%XCTV,CSTURB%XCHV : cts for the T and moisture variances
145!!
146!! Module MODD_PARAMETERS
147!!
148!! JPVEXT_TURB : number of vertical external points
149!! JPHEXT : number of horizontal external points
150!!

265
266!
267REAL, DIMENSION(D%NIJT,KSV), INTENT(IN) :: PSFSVM ! t - deltat
268!

287 ! in the temporal implicit scheme (also used to store coefficient
288 ! J in Section 5)
289 zres, & ! guess of the treated variable at t+ deltat when the turbu-
290 ! lence is the only source of evolution added to the ones
291 ! considered in ZSOURCE
292 zflxz, & ! vertical flux of the treated variable
293 zsource, & ! source of evolution for the treated variable
294 zkeff, & ! effectif diffusion coeff = LT * SQRT( TKE )
295 zwork1,zwork2,&
296 zwork3,zwork4,&! working var. for shuman operators (array syntax)
297 zmzmrhodj
298INTEGER :: IKT ! array size in k direction

303INTEGER :: JIJ,JK ! loop

308REAL :: ZCSV !constant for the scalar flux

311REAL(KIND=jphook) :: zhook_handle

313!----------------------------------------------------------------------------
314!
315!* 1. PRELIMINARIES
316! -------------
317!
318IF (lhook) CALL dr_hook('TURB_VER_SV_FLUX',0,zhook_handle)
319!
320ikt=d%NKT
321iktb=d%NKTB
322ikte=d%NKTE
323ikb=d%NKB
324ike=d%NKE
325ika=d%NKA
326ikl=d%NKL
327iije=d%NIJE
328iijb=d%NIJB
329!
330IF (turbn%LHARAT) THEN
331!$acc kernels
332 !$mnh_expand_array(JIJ=IIJB:IIJE,JK=1:IKT)
333 zkeff(iijb:iije,1:ikt) = plm(iijb:iije,1:ikt) * sqrt(ptkem(iijb:iije,1:ikt))
334 !$mnh_end_expand_array(JIJ=IIJB:IIJE,JK=1:IKT)
335!$acc end kernels
336ELSE
337!$acc kernels
338 !$mnh_expand_array(JIJ=IIJB:IIJE,JK=1:IKT)
339 zwork1(iijb:iije,1:ikt) = plm(iijb:iije,1:ikt)*sqrt(ptkem(iijb:iije,1:ikt))
340 !$mnh_end_expand_array(JIJ=IIJB:IIJE,JK=1:IKT)
341!$acc end kernels
342 CALL mzm_phy(d,zwork1,zkeff)
343ENDIF
344!
345IF(oblowsnow) THEN
346! See Vionnet (PhD, 2012) for a complete discussion around the value of the Schmidt number for blowing snow variables
347 zcsv=turbn%XCHF/prsnow
348ELSE
349 zcsv=turbn%XCHF
350ENDIF
351!----------------------------------------------------------------------------
352!
353!* 8. SOURCES OF PASSIVE SCALAR VARIABLES
354! -----------------------------------
355!
356CALL mzm_phy(d,prhodj,zmzmrhodj)
357DO jsv=1,ksv
358!
359 IF (onomixlg .AND. jsv >= ksv_lgbeg .AND. jsv<= ksv_lgend) cycle
360!
361! Preparation of the arguments for TRIDIAG
362 IF (turbn%LHARAT) THEN
363!$acc kernels
364 !$mnh_expand_array(JIJ=IIJB:IIJE,JK=1:IKT)
365 za(iijb:iije,1:ikt) = -ptstep * zkeff(iijb:iije,1:ikt) * zmzmrhodj(iijb:iije,1:ikt) &
366 / pdzz(iijb:iije,1:ikt)**2
367 !$mnh_end_expand_array(JIJ=IIJB:IIJE,JK=1:IKT)
368!$acc end kernels
369 ELSE
370!$acc kernels
371 !$mnh_expand_array(JIJ=IIJB:IIJE,JK=1:IKT)
372 za(iijb:iije,1:ikt) = -ptstep*zcsv*ppsi_sv(iijb:iije,1:ikt,jsv) * &
373 zkeff(iijb:iije,1:ikt) * zmzmrhodj(iijb:iije,1:ikt) / pdzz(iijb:iije,1:ikt)**2
374 !$mnh_end_expand_array(JIJ=IIJB:IIJE,JK=1:IKT)
375!$acc end kernels
376 ENDIF
377!$acc kernels
378 !$mnh_expand_array(JIJ=IIJB:IIJE,JK=1:IKT)
379 zsource(iijb:iije,1:ikt) = 0.
380 !$mnh_end_expand_array(JIJ=IIJB:IIJE,JK=1:IKT)
381!$acc end kernels
382!
383! Compute the sources for the JSVth scalar variable
384
385!* in 3DIM case, a part of the flux goes vertically, and another goes horizontally
386! (in presence of slopes)
387!* in 1DIM case, the part of energy released in horizontal flux
388! is taken into account in the vertical part
389 IF (turbn%CTURBDIM=='3DIM') THEN
390!$acc kernels
391 !$mnh_expand_array(JIJ=IIJB:IIJE)
392 zsource(iijb:iije,ikb) = (turbn%XIMPL*psfsvp(iijb:iije,jsv) + pexpl*psfsvm(iijb:iije,jsv)) / &
393 pdzz(iijb:iije,ikb) * pdircoszw(iijb:iije) &
394 * 0.5 * (1. + prhodj(iijb:iije,ika) / prhodj(iijb:iije,ikb))
395 !$mnh_end_expand_array(JIJ=IIJB:IIJE)
396!$acc end kernels
397 ELSE
398!$acc kernels
399 !$mnh_expand_array(JIJ=IIJB:IIJE)
400 zsource(iijb:iije,ikb) = (turbn%XIMPL*psfsvp(iijb:iije,jsv) + pexpl*psfsvm(iijb:iije,jsv)) / &
401 pdzz(iijb:iije,ikb) / pdircoszw(iijb:iije) &
402 * 0.5 * (1. + prhodj(iijb:iije,ika) / prhodj(iijb:iije,ikb))
403 !$mnh_end_expand_array(JIJ=IIJB:IIJE)
404!$acc end kernels
405 END IF
406!$acc kernels
407 zsource(iijb:iije,iktb+1:ikte-1) = 0.
408 zsource(iijb:iije,ike) = 0.
409!$acc end kernels
410!
411! Obtention of the split JSV scalar variable at t+ deltat
412 CALL tridiag(d,psvm(:,:,jsv),za,ptstep,pexpl,turbn%XIMPL,prhodj,zsource,zres)
413!
414! Compute the equivalent tendency for the JSV scalar variable
415!$acc kernels
416 !$mnh_expand_array(JIJ=IIJB:IIJE,JK=1:IKT)
417 prsvs(iijb:iije,1:ikt,jsv)= prsvs(iijb:iije,1:ikt,jsv)+ &
418 prhodj(iijb:iije,1:ikt)*(zres(iijb:iije,1:ikt)-psvm(iijb:iije,1:ikt,jsv))/ptstep
419 !$mnh_end_expand_array(JIJ=IIJB:IIJE,JK=1:IKT)
420!$acc end kernels
421!
422 IF ( (turbn%LTURB_FLX .AND. tpfile%LOPENED) .OR. tles%LLES_CALL .OR. oflyer ) THEN
423 ! Diagnostic of the cartesian vertical flux
424 !
425 zflxz(:,:) = -zcsv * ppsi_sv(:,:,jsv) * mzm(plm*sqrt(ptkem)) / pdzz * &
426 dzm( turbn%XIMPL*zres(:,:) + pexpl*psvm(:,:,jsv) )
427 ! surface flux
428 !* in 3DIM case, a part of the flux goes vertically, and another goes horizontally
429 ! (in presence of slopes)
430 !* in 1DIM case, the part of energy released in horizontal flux
431 ! is taken into account in the vertical part
432 IF (turbn%CTURBDIM=='3DIM') THEN
433!$acc kernels
434 !$mnh_expand_array(JIJ=IIJB:IIJE)
435 zflxz(iijb:iije,ikb) = (turbn%XIMPL*psfsvp(iijb:iije,jsv) + pexpl*psfsvm(iijb:iije,jsv)) &
436 * pdircoszw(iijb:iije)
437 !$mnh_end_expand_array(JIJ=IIJB:IIJE)
438!$acc end kernels
439 ELSE
440!$acc kernels
441 !$mnh_expand_array(JIJ=IIJB:IIJE)
442 zflxz(iijb:iije,ikb) = (turbn%XIMPL*psfsvp(iijb:iije,jsv) + pexpl*psfsvm(iijb:iije,jsv)) &
443 / pdircoszw(iijb:iije)
444 !$mnh_end_expand_array(JIJ=IIJB:IIJE)
445!$acc end kernels
446 END IF
447 ! extrapolates the flux under the ground so that the vertical average with
448 ! the IKB flux gives the ground value
449 !
450!$acc kernels
451 !$mnh_expand_array(JIJ=IIJB:IIJE)
452 zflxz(iijb:iije,ika) = zflxz(iijb:iije,ikb)
453 !$mnh_end_expand_array(JIJ=IIJB:IIJE)
454!$acc end kernels
455 IF ( oflyer ) THEN
456!$acc kernels
457 DO jk=iktb+1,ikte-1
458 !$mnh_expand_array(JIJ=IIJB:IIJE)
459 pwsv(iijb:iije,jk,jsv)=0.5*(zflxz(iijb:iije,jk)+zflxz(iijb:iije,jk+ikl))
460 !$mnh_end_expand_array(JIJ=IIJB:IIJE)
461 END DO
462 !$mnh_expand_array(JIJ=IIJB:IIJE)
463 pwsv(iijb:iije,ikb,jsv)=0.5*(zflxz(iijb:iije,ikb)+zflxz(iijb:iije,ikb+ikl))
464 pwsv(iijb:iije,ike,jsv)=pwsv(iijb:iije,ike-ikl,jsv)
465 !$mnh_end_expand_array(JIJ=IIJB:IIJE)
466!$acc end kernels
467 END IF
468 END IF
469 !
470 IF (turbn%LTURB_FLX .AND. tpfile%LOPENED) THEN
471 ! stores the JSVth vertical flux
472 WRITE(ymnhname,'("WSV_FLX_",I3.3)') jsv
473 tzfield = tfieldmetadata( &
474 cmnhname = trim( ymnhname ), &
475 cstdname = '', &
476 clongname = trim( ymnhname ), &
477 cunits = 'SVUNIT m s-1', &
478 cdir = 'XY', &
479 ccomment = 'X_Y_Z_' // trim( ymnhname ), &
480 ngrid = 4, &
481 ntype = typereal, &
482 ndims = 3, &
483 ltimedep = .true. )
484 !
485!$acc update self(ZFLXZ)
486 CALL io_field_write_phy(d,tpfile,tzfield,zflxz)
487 END IF
488 !
489 ! Storage in the LES configuration
490 !
491 IF (tles%LLES_CALL) THEN
492 CALL second_mnh(ztime1)
493 CALL les_mean_subgrid_phy(d, tles, mzf(zflxz), tles%X_LES_SUBGRID_WSv(:,:,:,jsv) )
494 CALL les_mean_subgrid_phy(d, tles, gz_w_m(pwm,pdzz)*mzf(zflxz), &
495 tles%X_LES_RES_ddxa_W_SBG_UaSv(:,:,:,jsv) )
496 CALL les_mean_subgrid_phy(d, tles, mzf(gz_m_w(psvm(:,:,jsv),pdzz)*zflxz), &
497 tles%X_LES_RES_ddxa_Sv_SBG_UaSv(:,:,:,jsv) )
498 CALL les_mean_subgrid_phy(d, tles, -zcsvp*sqrt(ptkem)/plm*mzf(zflxz), tles%X_LES_SUBGRID_SvPz(:,:,:,jsv) )
499 CALL les_mean_subgrid_phy(d, tles, mzf(pwm*zflxz), tles%X_LES_RES_W_SBG_WSv(:,:,:,jsv) )
500 CALL second_mnh(ztime2)
501 tles%XTIME_LES = tles%XTIME_LES + ztime2 - ztime1
502 END IF
503 !
504END DO ! end of scalar loop
505!
506!----------------------------------------------------------------------------
507!
