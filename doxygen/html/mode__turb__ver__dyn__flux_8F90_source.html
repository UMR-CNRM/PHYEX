23!!**** *TURB_VER_DYN_FLUX* -compute the source terms due to the vertical turbulent
24!! fluxes.
25!!
26!! PURPOSE
27!! -------
28! The purpose of this routine is to compute the vertical turbulent
29! fluxes of the evolutive variables and give back the source
30! terms to the main program. In the case of large horizontal meshes,
31! the divergence of these vertical turbulent fluxes represent the whole
32! effect of the turbulence but when the three-dimensionnal version of
33! the turbulence scheme is activated (CTURBDIM="3DIM"), these divergences
34! are completed in the next routine TURB_HOR.
35! An arbitrary degree of implicitness has been implemented for the
36! temporal treatment of these diffusion terms.
37! The vertical boundary conditions are as follows:
38! * at the bottom, the surface fluxes are prescribed at the same
39! as the other turbulent fluxes
40! * at the top, the turbulent fluxes are set to 0.
41! It should be noted that the condensation has been implicitely included
42! in this turbulence scheme by using conservative variables and computing
43! the subgrid variance of a statistical variable s indicating the presence
44! or not of condensation in a given mesh.
45!
46!!** METHOD
47!! ------
48!! 1D type calculations are made;
49!! The vertical turbulent fluxes are computed in an off-centered
50!! implicit scheme (a Crank-Nicholson type with coefficients different
51!! than 0.5), which allows to vary the degree of implicitness of the
52!! formulation.
53!! The different prognostic variables are treated one by one.
54!! The contributions of each turbulent fluxes are cumulated into the
55!! tendency PRvarS, and into the dynamic and thermal production of
56!! TKE if necessary.
57!!
58!! In section 2 and 3, the thermodynamical fields are considered.
59!! Only the turbulent fluxes of the conservative variables
60!! (Thetal and Rnp stored in PRx(:,:,:,1)) are computed.
61!! Note that the turbulent fluxes at the vertical
62!! boundaries are given either by the soil scheme for the surface one
63!! ( at the same instant as the others fluxes) and equal to 0 at the
64!! top of the model. The thermal production is computed by vertically
65!! averaging the turbulent flux and multiply this flux at the mass point by
66!! a function ETHETA or EMOIST, which preform the transformation from the
67!! conservative variables to the virtual potential temperature.
68!!
69!! In section 4, the variance of the statistical variable
70!! s indicating presence or not of condensation, is determined in function
71!! of the turbulent moments of the conservative variables and its
72!! squarred root is stored in PSIGS. This information will be completed in
73!! the horizontal turbulence if the turbulence dimensionality is not
74!! equal to "1DIM".
75!!
76!! In section 5, the x component of the stress tensor is computed.
77!! The surface flux <u'w'> is computed from the value of the surface
78!! fluxes computed in axes linked to the orography ( i", j" , k"):
79!! i" is parallel to the surface and in the direction of the maximum
80!! slope
81!! j" is also parallel to the surface and in the normal direction of
82!! the maximum slope
83!! k" is the normal to the surface
84!! In order to prevent numerical instability, the implicit scheme has
85!! been extended to the surface flux regarding to its dependence in
86!! function of U. The dependence in function of the other components
87!! introduced by the different rotations is only explicit.
88!! The turbulent fluxes are used to compute the dynamic production of
89!! TKE. For the last TKE level ( located at PDZZ(:,:,IKB)/2 from the
90!! ground), an harmonic extrapolation from the dynamic production at
91!! PDZZ(:,:,IKB) is used to avoid an evaluation of the gradient of U
92!! in the surface layer.
93!!
94!! In section 6, the same steps are repeated but for the y direction
95!! and in section 7, a diagnostic computation of the W variance is
96!! performed.
97!!
98!! In section 8, the turbulent fluxes for the scalar variables are
99!! computed by the same way as the conservative thermodynamical variables
100!!
101!!
102!! EXTERNAL
103!! --------
104!! GX_U_M, GY_V_M, GZ_W_M : cartesian gradient operators
105!! GX_U_UW,GY_V_VW (X,Y,Z) represent the direction of the gradient
106!! _(M,U,...)_ represent the localization of the
107!! field to be derivated
108!! _(M,UW,...) represent the localization of the
109!! field derivated
110!!
111!!
112!! MXM,MXF,MYM,MYF,MZM,MZF
113!! : Shuman functions (mean operators)
114!! DXF,DYF,DZF,DZM
115!! : Shuman functions (difference operators)
116!!
117!! SUBROUTINE TRIDIAG_WIND: to compute the split implicit evolution
118!! of a variable located at a wind point
119!!
120!! FUNCTIONs ETHETA and EMOIST :
121!! allows to compute:
122!! - the coefficients for the turbulent correlation between
123!! any variable and the virtual potential temperature, of its
124!! correlations with the conservative potential temperature and
125!! the humidity conservative variable:
126!! ------- ------- -------
127!! A' Thv' = ETHETA A' Thl' + EMOIST A' Rnp'
128!!
129!!
130!! IMPLICIT ARGUMENTS
131!! ------------------
132!! Module MODD_CST : contains physical constants
133!!
134!! CST%XG : gravity constant
135!!
136!! Module MODD_CTURB: contains the set of constants for
137!! the turbulence scheme
138!!
139!! CSTURB%XCMFS,XCMFB : cts for the momentum flux
140!! CSTURB%XCSHF : ct for the sensible heat flux
141!! CSTURB%XCHF : ct for the moisture flux
142!! CSTURB%XCTV,CSTURB%XCHV : cts for the T and moisture variances
143!!
144!! Module MODD_PARAMETERS
145!!
146!! JPVEXT_TURB : number of vertical external points
147!! JPHEXT : number of horizontal external points

344!* 1. PRELIMINARIES
345! -------------
346REAL(KIND=jphook) :: zhook_handle
347IF (lhook) CALL dr_hook('TURB_VER_DYN_FLUX',0,zhook_handle)
348!
349ikt=d%NKT
350iktb=d%NKTB
351ikte=d%NKTE
352ikb=d%NKB
353ike=d%NKE
354ika=d%NKA
355iku=d%NKU
356ikl=d%NKL
357iije=d%NIJE
358iijb=d%NIJB
359!
360gocean= oocean
361!
362!$acc kernels present_cr(ZA,ZSOURCE)
363za(:,:)=xundef
364pdp(:,:)=xundef
365!
366zsource(:,:) = 0.
367zflxz(:,:) = 0.
368zcmfs = csturb%XCMFS
369IF (turbn%LHARAT) zcmfs=1.
370!
371!$mnh_expand_array(JIJ=IIJB:IIJE)
372zdirsinzw(iijb:iije) = sqrt(1.-pdircoszw(iijb:iije)**2)
373!$mnh_end_expand_array(JIJ=IIJB:IIJE)
374!$acc end kernels
375! compute the coefficients for the uncentred gradient computation near the
376! ground
377!
378! With TURBN%LHARATU length scale and TKE are at half levels so remove MZM
379!
380IF (turbn%LHARAT) THEN
381!$acc kernels
382 !$mnh_expand_array(JIJ=IIJB:IIJE,JK=1:IKT)
383 zkeff(iijb:iije,1:ikt) = plm(iijb:iije,1:ikt) * sqrt(ptkem(iijb:iije,1:ikt))
384 !$mnh_end_expand_array(JIJ=IIJB:IIJE,JK=1:IKT)
385!$acc end kernels
386ELSE
387!$acc kernels
388 !$mnh_expand_array(JIJ=IIJB:IIJE,JK=1:IKT)
389 zwork1(iijb:iije,1:ikt) = plm(iijb:iije,1:ikt) * sqrt(ptkem(iijb:iije,1:ikt))
390 !$mnh_end_expand_array(JIJ=IIJB:IIJE,JK=1:IKT)
391!$acc end kernels
392 CALL mzm_phy(d,zwork1,zkeff)
393ENDIF
394!
395!$acc kernels
396zuslopem(iijb:iije)=puslopem(iijb:iije)
397zvslopem(iijb:iije)=pvslopem(iijb:iije)
398zfluxsfcu(iijb:iije)=psfum(iijb:iije)
399zfluxsfcv(iijb:iije)=psfvm(iijb:iije)
400!$acc end kernels
401!
402!----------------------------------------------------------------------------
403!
404!
405!* 5. SOURCES OF U,W WIND COMPONENTS AND PARTIAL DYNAMIC PRODUCTION
406! -------------------------------------------------------------
407!
408!* 5.1 Source of U wind component
409!
410! Preparation of the arguments for TRIDIAG_WIND
411!
412za(:,:) = -ptstep * zcmfs * mxm( zkeff(:,:) ) * &
413 mxm(mzm( prhodj(:,:) )) / mxm(pdzz(:,:))**2
414!
415!
416! Compute the source of U wind component
417!
418! compute the coefficient between the vertical flux and the 2 components of the
419! wind following the slope
420!$acc kernels
421!$mnh_expand_array(JIJ=IIJB:IIJE)
422zcoefflxu(iijb:iije) = pcdueff(iijb:iije) * (pdircoszw(iijb:iije)**2 - zdirsinzw(iijb:iije)**2) &
423 * pcosslope(iijb:iije)
424zcoefflxv(iijb:iije) = pcdueff(iijb:iije) * pdircoszw(iijb:iije) * psinslope(iijb:iije)
425!
426! prepare the implicit scheme coefficients for the surface flux
427zcoefs(iijb:iije)= zcoefflxu(iijb:iije) * pcosslope(iijb:iije) * pdircoszw(iijb:iije) &
428 +zcoefflxv(iijb:iije) * psinslope(iijb:iije)
429!
430! average this flux to be located at the U,W vorticity point
431!$mnh_end_expand_array(JIJ=IIJB:IIJE)
432!$acc end kernels
433!
434zcoefs(:)=mxm(zcoefs(:) / pdzz(:,ikb) )
435!
436!
437!$acc kernels
438!$mnh_expand_array(JIJ=IIJB:IIJE,JK=IKTB+1:IKTE-1)
439zsource(:,iktb+1:ikte-1) = 0.
440!$mnh_end_expand_array(JIJ=IIJB:IIJE,JK=IKTB+1:IKTE-1)
441!$acc end kernels
442! ZSOURCE= sfc FLUX /DZ
443! Sfx flux assumed to be in SI & at vorticity point
444!
445IF (gocean) THEN ! Ocean model
446 zsource(:,ike) = mxm(zfluxsfcu(:)/pdzz(:,ike)) &
447 *0.5 * ( 1. + mxm(prhodj(:,iku)) / mxm(prhodj(:,ike)))
448 !
449 ! Zero flux at the ocean domain bottom
450!$acc kernels
451!$mnh_expand_array(JIJ=IIJB:IIJE)
452 zsource(:,ikb) = 0.
453!$mnh_end_expand_array(JIJ=IIJB:IIJE)
454!$acc end kernels
455 !
456ELSE ! Atmosphere
457 ! Compute the explicit tangential flux at the W point
458!$acc kernels
459 !$mnh_expand_array(JIJ=IIJB:IIJE)
460 zsource(:,ikb) = &
461 ptau11m(:) * pcosslope(:) * pdircoszw(:) * zdirsinzw(:) &
462 -ptau12m(:) * psinslope(:) * zdirsinzw(:) &
463 -ptau33m(:) * pcosslope(:) * zdirsinzw(:) * pdircoszw(:)
464 !$mnh_end_expand_array(JIJ=IIJB:IIJE)
465!$acc end kernels
466!
467 ! add the vertical part or the surface flux at the U,W vorticity point
468!
469 zsource(:,ikb) = &
470 ( mxm( zsource(:,ikb) / pdzz(:,ikb) ) &
471 + mxm( zcoefflxu(:) / pdzz(:,ikb) &
472 *zuslopem(:) &
473 -zcoefflxv(:) / pdzz(:,ikb) &
474 *zvslopem(:) ) &
475 - zcoefs(:) * pum(:,ikb) * turbn%XIMPL &
476 ) * 0.5 * ( 1. + mxm(prhodj(:,ika)) / mxm(prhodj(:,ikb)) )
477!
478!$acc kernels
479 !$mnh_expand_array(JIJ=IIJB:IIJE)
480 zsource(:,ike) = 0.
481 !$mnh_end_expand_array(JIJ=IIJB:IIJE)
482!$acc end kernels
483ENDIF
484!
485! Obtention of the split U at t+ deltat
486!
487CALL tridiag_wind(d,pum,za,zcoefs,ptstep,pexpl,turbn%XIMPL, &
488 mxm(prhodj),zsource,zres)
489!
490! Compute the equivalent tendency for the U wind component
491!
492prus(:,:)=prus(:,:)+mxm(prhodj(:,:))*(zres(:,:)-pum(:,:))/ptstep
493!
494!* 5.2 Partial TKE Dynamic Production
495!
496! vertical flux of the U wind component
497!
498zflxz(:,:) = -zcmfs * mxm(zkeff(:,:)) * &
499 dzm(turbn%XIMPL*zres(:,:) + pexpl*pum(:,:)) / mxm(pdzz(:,:))
500!
501IF (gocean) THEN
502!$acc kernels
503 !$mnh_expand_array(JIJ=IIJB:IIJE)
504 zflxz(:,ike+1) = zfluxsfcu(:)
505 !$mnh_end_expand_array(JIJ=IIJB:IIJE)
506!$acc end kernels
507ELSE
508 ! surface flux
509 zflxz(:,ikb) = mxm(pdzz(:,ikb)) * &
510 ( zsource(:,ikb) &
511 +zcoefs(:) * zres(:,ikb) * turbn%XIMPL &
512 ) / 0.5 / ( 1. + mxm(prhodj(:,ika)) / mxm(prhodj(:,ikb)) )
