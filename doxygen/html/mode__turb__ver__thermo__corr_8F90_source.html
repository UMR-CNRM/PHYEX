27 !!**** *TURB_VER_THERMO_FLUX* -compute the source terms due to the vertical turbulent
28 !! fluxes.
29 !!
30 !! PURPOSE
31 !! -------
32 ! The purpose of this routine is to compute the vertical turbulent
33 ! fluxes of the evolutive variables and give back the source
34 ! terms to the main program. In the case of large horizontal meshes,
35 ! the divergence of these vertical turbulent fluxes represent the whole
36 ! effect of the turbulence but when the three-dimensionnal version of
37 ! the turbulence scheme is activated (CTURBDIM="3DIM"), these divergences
38 ! are completed in the next routine TURB_HOR.
39 ! An arbitrary degree of implicitness has been implemented for the
40 ! temporal treatment of these diffusion terms.
41 ! The vertical boundary conditions are as follows:
42 ! * at the bottom, the surface fluxes are prescribed at the same
43 ! as the other turbulent fluxes
44 ! * at the top, the turbulent fluxes are set to 0.
45 ! It should be noted that the condensation has been implicitely included
46 ! in this turbulence scheme by using conservative variables and computing
47 ! the subgrid variance of a statistical variable s indicating the presence
48 ! or not of condensation in a given mesh.
49 !
50 !!** METHOD
51 !! ------
52 !! 1D type calculations are made;
53 !! The vertical turbulent fluxes are computed in an off-centered
54 !! implicit scheme (a Crank-Nicholson type with coefficients different
55 !! than 0.5), which allows to vary the degree of implicitness of the
56 !! formulation.
57 !! The different prognostic variables are treated one by one.
58 !! The contributions of each turbulent fluxes are cumulated into the
59 !! tendency PRvarS, and into the dynamic and thermal production of
60 !! TKE if necessary.
61 !!
62 !! In section 2 and 3, the thermodynamical fields are considered.
63 !! Only the turbulent fluxes of the conservative variables
64 !! (Thetal and Rnp stored in PRx(:,:,:,1)) are computed.
65 !! Note that the turbulent fluxes at the vertical
66 !! boundaries are given either by the soil scheme for the surface one
67 !! ( at the same instant as the others fluxes) and equal to 0 at the
68 !! top of the model. The thermal production is computed by vertically
69 !! averaging the turbulent flux and multiply this flux at the mass point by
70 !! a function ETHETA or EMOIST, which preform the transformation from the
71 !! conservative variables to the virtual potential temperature.
72 !!
73 !! In section 4, the variance of the statistical variable
74 !! s indicating presence or not of condensation, is determined in function
75 !! of the turbulent moments of the conservative variables and its
76 !! squarred root is stored in PSIGS. This information will be completed in
77 !! the horizontal turbulence if the turbulence dimensionality is not
78 !! equal to "1DIM".
79 !!
80 !! In section 5, the x component of the stress tensor is computed.
81 !! The surface flux <u'w'> is computed from the value of the surface
82 !! fluxes computed in axes linked to the orography ( i", j" , k"):
83 !! i" is parallel to the surface and in the direction of the maximum
84 !! slope
85 !! j" is also parallel to the surface and in the normal direction of
86 !! the maximum slope
87 !! k" is the normal to the surface
88 !! In order to prevent numerical instability, the implicit scheme has
89 !! been extended to the surface flux regarding to its dependence in
90 !! function of U. The dependence in function of the other components
91 !! introduced by the different rotations is only explicit.
92 !! The turbulent fluxes are used to compute the dynamic production of
93 !! TKE. For the last TKE level ( located at PDZZ(:,:,IKB)/2 from the
94 !! ground), an harmonic extrapolation from the dynamic production at
95 !! PDZZ(:,:,IKB) is used to avoid an evaluation of the gradient of U
96 !! in the surface layer.
97 !!
98 !! In section 6, the same steps are repeated but for the y direction
99 !! and in section 7, a diagnostic computation of the W variance is
100 !! performed.
101 !!
102 !! In section 8, the turbulent fluxes for the scalar variables are
103 !! computed by the same way as the conservative thermodynamical variables
104 !!
105 !!
106 !! EXTERNAL
107 !! --------
108 !! GX_U_M, GY_V_M, GZ_W_M : cartesian gradient operators
109 !! GX_U_UW,GY_V_VW (X,Y,Z) represent the direction of the gradient
110 !! _(M,U,...)_ represent the localization of the
111 !! field to be derivated
112 !! _(M,UW,...) represent the localization of the
113 !! field derivated
114 !!
115 !!
116 !! MXM,MXF,MYM,MYF,MZM,MZF
117 !! : Shuman functions (mean operators)
118 !! DXF,DYF,DZF,DZM
119 !! : Shuman functions (difference operators)
120 !!
121 !! FUNCTIONs ETHETA and EMOIST :
122 !! allows to compute:
123 !! - the coefficients for the turbulent correlation between
124 !! any variable and the virtual potential temperature, of its
125 !! correlations with the conservative potential temperature and
126 !! the humidity conservative variable:
127 !! ------- ------- -------
128 !! A' Thv' = ETHETA A' Thl' + EMOIST A' Rnp'
129 !!
