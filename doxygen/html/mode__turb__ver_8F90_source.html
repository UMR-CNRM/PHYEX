33!!**** *TURB_VER* -compute the source terms due to the vertical turbulent
34!! fluxes.
35!!
36!! PURPOSE
37!! -------
38! The purpose of this routine is to compute the vertical turbulent
39! fluxes of the evolutive variables and give back the source
40! terms to the main program. In the case of large horizontal meshes,
41! the divergence of these vertical turbulent fluxes represent the whole
42! effect of the turbulence but when the three-dimensionnal version of
43! the turbulence scheme is activated (CTURBDIM="3DIM"), these divergences
44! are completed in the next routine TURB_HOR.
45! An arbitrary degree of implicitness has been implemented for the
46! temporal treatment of these diffusion terms.
47! The vertical boundary conditions are as follows:
48! * at the bottom, the surface fluxes are prescribed at the same
49! as the other turbulent fluxes
50! * at the top, the turbulent fluxes are set to 0.
51! It should be noted that the condensation has been implicitely included
52! in this turbulence scheme by using conservative variables and computing
53! the subgrid variance of a statistical variable s indicating the presence
54! or not of condensation in a given mesh.
55!
56!!** METHOD
57!! ------
58!! 1D type calculations are made;
59!! The vertical turbulent fluxes are computed in an off-centered
60!! implicit scheme (a Crank-Nicholson type with coefficients different
61!! than 0.5), which allows to vary the degree of implicitness of the
62!! formulation.
63!! The different prognostic variables are treated one by one.
64!! The contributions of each turbulent fluxes are cumulated into the
65!! tendency PRvarS, and into the dynamic and thermal production of
66!! TKE if necessary.
67!!
68!! In section 2 and 3, the thermodynamical fields are considered.
69!! Only the turbulent fluxes of the conservative variables
70!! (Thetal and Rnp stored in PRx(:,:,:,1)) are computed.
71!! Note that the turbulent fluxes at the vertical
72!! boundaries are given either by the soil scheme for the surface one
73!! ( at the same instant as the others fluxes) and equal to 0 at the
74!! top of the model. The thermal production is computed by vertically
75!! averaging the turbulent flux and multiply this flux at the mass point by
76!! a function ETHETA or EMOIST, which preform the transformation from the
77!! conservative variables to the virtual potential temperature.
78!!
79!! In section 4, the variance of the statistical variable
80!! s indicating presence or not of condensation, is determined in function
81!! of the turbulent moments of the conservative variables and its
82!! squarred root is stored in PSIGS. This information will be completed in
83!! the horizontal turbulence if the turbulence dimensionality is not
84!! equal to "1DIM".
85!!
86!! In section 5, the x component of the stress tensor is computed.
87!! The surface flux <u'w'> is computed from the value of the surface
88!! fluxes computed in axes linked to the orography ( i", j" , k"):
89!! i" is parallel to the surface and in the direction of the maximum
90!! slope
91!! j" is also parallel to the surface and in the normal direction of
92!! the maximum slope
93!! k" is the normal to the surface
94!! In order to prevent numerical instability, the implicit scheme has
95!! been extended to the surface flux regarding to its dependence in
96!! function of U. The dependence in function of the other components
97!! introduced by the different rotations is only explicit.
98!! The turbulent fluxes are used to compute the dynamic production of
99!! TKE. For the last TKE level ( located at PDZZ(:,:,IKB)/2 from the
100!! ground), an harmonic extrapolation from the dynamic production at
101!! PDZZ(:,:,IKB) is used to avoid an evaluation of the gradient of U
102!! in the surface layer.
103!!
104!! In section 6, the same steps are repeated but for the y direction
105!! and in section 7, a diagnostic computation of the W variance is
106!! performed.
107!!
108!! In section 8, the turbulent fluxes for the scalar variables are
109!! computed by the same way as the conservative thermodynamical variables
110!!
111!!
112!! EXTERNAL
113!! --------
114!! GX_U_M, GY_V_M, GZ_W_M : cartesian gradient operators
115!! GX_U_UW,GY_V_VW (X,Y,Z) represent the direction of the gradient
116!! _(M,U,...)_ represent the localization of the
117!! field to be derivated
118!! _(M,UW,...) represent the localization of the
119!! field derivated
120!!
121!! SUBROUTINE TRIDIAG : to compute the split implicit evolution
122!! of a variable located at a mass point
123!!
124!! SUBROUTINE TRIDIAG_WIND: to compute the split implicit evolution
125!! of a variable located at a wind point
126!!
127!! FUNCTIONs ETHETA and EMOIST :
128!! allows to compute:
129!! - the coefficients for the turbulent correlation between
130!! any variable and the virtual potential temperature, of its
131!! correlations with the conservative potential temperature and
132!! the humidity conservative variable:
133!! ------- ------- -------
134!! A' Thv' = ETHETA A' Thl' + EMOIST A' Rnp'
135!!
136!!
137!! IMPLICIT ARGUMENTS
138!! ------------------
139!! Module MODD_CST : contains physical constants
140!!
141!! CST%XG : gravity constant
142!!
143!! Module MODD_CTURB: contains the set of constants for
144!! the turbulence scheme
145!!
146!! CSTURB%XCMFS,XCMFB : cts for the momentum flux
147!! CSTURB%XCSHF : ct for the sensible heat flux
148!! CSTURB%XCHF : ct for the moisture flux
149!! CSTURB%XCTV,CSTURB%XCHV : cts for the T and moisture variances

152!!
153!! JPVEXT_TURB : number of vertical external points
